"You don't get what you deserve. You get what you negotiate."

Before Negotiating

Know your BATNA. Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement. Your walk-away point.
Research market rates. Know what's fair before you ask.
Understand their constraints. What do they need? What limits them?
Prepare your case. Facts and examples, not feelings.
Practice out loud. Rehearse responses to objections.

During Negotiation

Let them go first. You might be surprised (higher or lower).
Anchor high (or low). First number shapes the conversation.
Use silence. After they speak, pause. They often improve their offer.
Never accept first offer. There's always room.
Ask "How did you arrive at that number?" Makes them justify.
Use "I" not "You". "I was hoping for X" not "You should give me X"
Get beyond positions to interests. What do they really want?

Salary Negotiation

Never give a number first. "I'm flexible - what's the range for this role?"
Use ranges, aim high. "I'm looking for $85-95K" (want $85K)
Negotiate more than salary. Bonus, vacation, remote work, title, start date.
Get it in writing. Before you accept.
Timing matters. After offer, before acceptance is your leverage peak.

Phrases That Work

"Based on my research..."
"I'm excited about this, and..."
"Is there flexibility on..."
"What would it take to get to X?"
"Can you help me understand..."
"I'd like to find a solution that works for both of us."
